  <h2 class="section-title">History of Sadaat</h2>

  <div class="quote-box" style="font-size:1.1rem;direction:rtl">
    وَمَن يُعَظِّمْ شَعَائِرَ اللَّهِ فَإِنَّهَا مِن تَقْوَى الْقُلُوبِ
  </div>

  <h3 class="section-title" style="font-size:1.2rem">Who Are the Sadaat?</h3>
  <p>Sadaat (singular: Sayyid) are descendants of the Holy Prophet Muhammad ﷺ through his daughter Sayyida Fatima al-Zahra (S.A) and son-in-law Imam Ali ibn Abi Talib (A.S). They carry the most noble lineage in human history — Ahl al-Bayt (A.S), the family of the Prophet.</p>

  <div class="info-box mt1">
    <h4>Major Sadat Branches</h4>
    <p><strong>Hasani:</strong> Through Imam Hasan al-Mujtaba (A.S) — includes Gilani, Tabatabai</p>
    <p><strong>Husseini:</strong> Through Imam Hussain (A.S) — includes Zaidi, Musavi/Kazemi, <strong>Rizvi/Razavi, Naqvi → Bukhari</strong>, Askari, Jafari, Gardezi, Tirmizi, Abedi</p>
  </div>

  <h3 class="section-title" style="font-size:1.2rem">Naqvi Bukhari — Our Lineage</h3>
  <p>The <strong>Naqvi</strong> Sadaat are direct descendants of <strong>Imam Ali al-Naqi al-Hadi (A.S)</strong>, the 10th Imam of the Twelver Shia. The <strong>Bukhari</strong> title comes from <strong>Bukhara (modern Uzbekistan)</strong>, the birthplace of Hazrat Jalaluddin Surkh Posh Bukhari (R.A) who migrated to the Indian subcontinent.</p>

  <h3 class="section-title" style="font-size:1.2rem">25 Naqvi Branches in Subcontinent</h3>
  <div style="display:grid;grid-template-columns:repeat(auto-fill,minmax(180px,1fr));gap:0.5rem;margin:1rem 0">
    <div id="naqvi-branches-list"></div>
  </div>

  <h3 class="section-title" style="font-size:1.2rem">Migration Route</h3>
  <div class="timeline">
    <div class="timeline-item">
      <div class="place">Arabia — Medina / Samarra</div>
      <div class="era">828–900 CE</div>
      <p>Imam Ali al-Naqi (A.S) lived in Samarra under Abbasid surveillance. His descendants after Syed Jafar Sani began their journey eastward. Some generations settled in Baghdad.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="timeline-item">
      <div class="place">Baghdad → Bukhara (Central Asia)</div>
      <div class="era">10th–12th Century CE</div>
      <p>Syed Abdullah Idrees Baghdadi's descendants migrated via Bukhara. Hazrat Jalaluddin Surkh Posh Bukhari born 1195 CE in Bukhara — took the Bukhari title.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="timeline-item">
      <div class="place">Uch Sharif (Punjab)</div>
      <div class="era">1244 CE</div>
      <p>Surkh Posh Bukhari (R.A) migrated to Uch Sharif, founded religious school. His tomb is UNESCO tentative World Heritage Site. Jahaniyan Jahangasht (1308–1384) continued the tradition.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="timeline-item">
      <div class="place">Lahore (Mughal Era)</div>
      <div class="era">16th–17th Century CE</div>
      <p>Syed Sultan Jalaluddin Haider Lahori (wafat 1016H/1607CE) settled in Lahore. Buried at Bibiyan Pak Damnan. Son: Syed Ilm ud Din. Despite Suhrawardi label imposed later — he was kattar Shia Ithna Ashari irfani buzurg.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="timeline-item">
      <div class="place">Hijaz (Makkah–Madinah) → Iraq → Iran</div>
      <div class="era">17th–19th Century CE</div>
      <p>After Syed Sultan Jalaluddin Haider Lahori, his descendants migrated for Hajj, Umra, and Ahl al-Bayt devotion to Hijaz, then some generations in Iraq and Iran (Safavi circles). Incomplete historical record due to travel and taqiya.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="timeline-item">
      <div class="place">Firozabad, Uttar Pradesh (India)</div>
      <div class="era">19th Century CE</div>
      <p>Syed Ameer Ali Shah — direct Arab hijrat to UP. Founded the Aal-e-Ameer branch. His son Syed Shakir Ali, then Syed Ashiq Ali, then Syed Nafees Ali (martyred — sectarian violence).</p>
    </div>
    <div class="timeline-item">
      <div class="place">Agra (Emergency Migration)</div>
      <div class="era">Late 19th Century CE</div>
      <p>After martyrdom of Syed Nafees Ali Shah and his wife, "Phuphi Amman" (sister of Nafees Ali) rescued his children overnight from Firozabad to Agra. This was the turning point of the family's survival.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="timeline-item">
      <div class="place">Hyderabad Sindh → Karachi (Pakistan)</div>
      <div class="era">1947 CE onwards</div>
      <p>After Partition, Syed Hafeez Ali and Syed Muhabbat Ali established the family prominently in Hyderabad Sindh. Subsequent generations in Karachi. Current generation: Syed Qadeer Ali Naqvi, Syed Shahzaib Ali Naqvi.</p>
    </div>
  </div>

  <h3 class="section-title" style="font-size:1.2rem">Maktab — Religious Identity</h3>
  <div class="emerald-box">
    <p><strong>This family follows exclusively Shia Ithna Ashari Usooli maktab.</strong> Despite various Sufi labels imposed on some ancestors in later eras, the entire chain has always been connected to the fiqh and shariat of Ahl al-Bayt (A.S).</p>
    <ul style="margin-top:0.8rem;padding-left:1.5rem;line-height:2">
      <li><strong>Surkh Posh Bukhari R.A.</strong> — Irfani buzurg. Sufi label: later imposed in Mughal era. Reality: Shia Ithna Ashari irfani.</li>
      <li><strong>Jahaniyan Jahangasht R.A.</strong> — Irfani buzurg with deep Sufi silasil connections but himself an irfani figure.</li>
      <li><strong>Syed Sultan Jalaluddin Haider Lahori R.A.</strong> — Kattar Shia Ithna Ashari. Suhrawardi label wrongly imposed. Entire nasl = Shia Ithna Ashari Usooli.</li>
    </ul>
  </div>

  <h3 class="section-title" style="font-size:1.2rem">Kundili Tradition</h3>
  <div class="info-box">
    <h4>یادگارِ کربلا — Kundili Filling</h4>
    <p>On the night of <strong>9th Muharram</strong>, each male member is given one date (khajoor) placed in a tray. This is called "Kundili Bharna." Consumed on 10th Muharram (Ashura). Symbolic remembrance of Imam Hussain's (A.S) companions on the night of Ashura, their collective sharing of food before martyrdom. Observed across: Firozabad, Agra, Lahore, Hyderabad Sindh, Karachi.</p>
  </div>

/* ═══ MEMBER FILTER ═══ */
function populateGenFilter() {
  const sel = document.getElementById('member-filter-gen');

function filterMembers() {
  const en = document.getElementById('member-search-en').value.toLowerCase();
  const ur = document.getElementById('member-search-ur').value;
  const gen = document.getElementById('member-filter-gen').value;
  const filtered = MEMBERS.filter(m => {
    const matchEn = !en || m.en.toLowerCase().includes(en);
    const matchUr = !ur || m.ur.includes(ur);
    const matchGen = !gen || m.gen == gen;
    return matchEn && matchUr && matchGen;
  });
  renderMembersGrid(filtered);
}
function clearSearch() {
  document.getElementById('member-search-en').value = '';
  document.getElementById('member-search-ur').value = '';
  document.getElementById('member-filter-gen').value = '';
  renderMembersGrid(MEMBERS);
}

/* ═══ MEMBER MODAL ═══ */
function openMemberModal(id) {
  const m = MEMBERS.find(x => x.id === id);
  if (!m) return;

  // Build ancestors path
  const path = [];
  let cur = m;
  while (cur) {
    path.unshift(cur.en);
    cur = cur.father ? MEMBERS.find(x => x.id === cur.father) : null;
    if (path.length > 15) break;
  }

  const links = [];
  if (m.wikishia) links.push(`<a href="${m.wikishia}" target="_blank" class="ext-link">📘 WikiShia (EN)</a>`);
  if (m.wikipedia) links.push(`<a href="${m.wikipedia}" target="_blank" class="ext-link">📖 Wikipedia (UR)</a>`);
  if (m.wikidata) links.push(`<a href="https://www.wikidata.org/wiki/${m.wikidata}" target="_blank" class="ext-link">📊 Wikidata: ${m.wikidata}</a>`);

let treeLayout = 'top-down';
let treeZoom;
let svg, g;

function buildTree() {
  const container = document.getElementById('tree-container');
  if (!container) return;
  const W = container.clientWidth || 900;
  const H = container.clientHeight || 600;

  d3.select('#tree-svg').selectAll('*').remove();
  svg = d3.select('#tree-svg').attr('width', W).attr('height', H);
  g = svg.append('g');

  treeZoom = d3.zoom()
    .scaleExtent([0.1, 3])
    .on('zoom', e => g.attr('transform', e.transform));
  svg.call(treeZoom);

  // Build hierarchy
  const rootData = buildHierarchy('imam-ali');
  const root = d3.hierarchy(rootData);

  // Collapse all beyond gen 5 initially
  root.descendants().forEach(d => {
    if (d.depth > 4 && d.children) {
      d._children = d.children;
      d.children = null;
    }
  });

  drawTree(root, W, H);

  // Controls
  document.getElementById('zoom-in').onclick = () => svg.transition().call(treeZoom.scaleBy, 1.4);
  document.getElementById('zoom-out').onclick = () => svg.transition().call(treeZoom.scaleBy, 0.7);
  document.getElementById('reset-view').onclick = () => svg.transition().call(treeZoom.transform, d3.zoomIdentity.translate(W/2, 60).scale(0.6));

  svg.call(treeZoom.transform, d3.zoomIdentity.translate(W/2, 60).scale(0.55));
}

function buildHierarchy(id, visited = new Set()) {
  if (visited.has(id)) return null;
  visited.add(id);
  const m = MEMBERS.find(x => x.id === id);
  if (!m) return null;
  const children = MEMBERS.filter(x => x.father === id)
    .map(c => buildHierarchy(c.id, visited))
    .filter(Boolean);
  return { id: m.id, name: m.en.length > 28 ? m.en.substring(0,26)+'…' : m.en, nameUr: m.ur, gen: m.gen, type: m.type, children: children.length ? children : null };
}

function drawTree(root, W, H) {
  const treeGen = d3.tree().nodeSize([120, 90]);
  treeGen(root);

  // Links
  g.selectAll('.link').data(root.links()).enter()
    .append('path').attr('class','link')
    .attr('fill','none')
    .attr('stroke','rgba(201,168,76,0.35)')
    .attr('stroke-width', 1.5)
    .attr('d', d3.linkVertical().x(d => d.x).y(d => d.y));

  // Nodes
  const node = g.selectAll('.node').data(root.descendants()).enter()
    .append('g')
    .attr('class','node')
    .attr('transform', d => `translate(${d.x},${d.y})`)
    .on('click', (e, d) => {
      if (d._children || d.children) {
        if (d.children) { d._children = d.children; d.children = null; }
        else { d.children = d._children; d._children = null; }
        g.selectAll('*').remove();
        drawTree(root, W, H);
      } else {
        openMemberModal(d.data.id);
      }
    })
    .on('mouseover', (e, d) => {
      const tip = document.getElementById('tree-tooltip');
      tip.style.display = 'block';
      tip.style.left = (e.offsetX + 12) + 'px';
      tip.style.top = (e.offsetY + 12) + 'px';
      tip.innerHTML = `<strong>${d.data.name}</strong><br><span style="font-family:var(--font-arabic);direction:rtl;display:block">${d.data.nameUr}</span><small>Gen ${d.data.gen} · ${d.data.type}</small>`;
    })
    .on('mouseout', () => { document.getElementById('tree-tooltip').style.display = 'none'; });

  const typeColors = {'imam':'#c0392b','wali':'#2d8a5e','founder':'#c9a84c','shaheed':'#8b1a1a','current':'#1a5c8b','ancestor':'#555','default':'#888'};

  node.append('circle')
    .attr('r', d => d.depth === 0 ? 14 : 10)
    .attr('fill', d => typeColors[d.data.type] || typeColors.default)
    .attr('stroke', d => d._children ? '#c9a84c' : 'rgba(255,255,255,0.3)')
    .attr('stroke-width', d => d._children ? 2.5 : 1);

  node.append('text')
    .attr('dy', d => d.children || d._children ? -18 : 18)
    .attr('text-anchor', 'middle')
    .attr('font-size', '9px')
    .attr('font-family', 'Crimson Text, serif')
    .attr('fill', '#e8c97a')
    .text(d => d.data.name.substring(0, 22));

  // Expand indicator
  node.filter(d => d._children)
    .append('text')
    .attr('dy', 4).attr('text-anchor','middle')
    .attr('font-size','10px').attr('fill','#c9a84c')
    .text('+');
}

function changeLayout(val) {
  treeLayout = val;
  buildTree();
}
function expandAll() {
  if (!svg) return;
  // Re-build without collapsing
  const rootData = buildHierarchy('imam-ali');
  const root = d3.hierarchy(rootData);
  const W = parseInt(svg.attr('width')), H = parseInt(svg.attr('height'));
  g.selectAll('*').remove();
  drawTree(root, W, H);
}
function collapseAll() { buildTree(); }

  const q = document.getElementById('tree-search').value.toLowerCase();
  if (!q) return;
  const found = MEMBERS.find(m => m.en.toLowerCase().includes(q) || m.ur.includes(q));
  if (found) openMemberModal(found.id);
}
