Historical Overview

This archive documents the historical genealogy, migration history, and lineage records of the Naqvi Bukhari Sayyid families of Hyderabad Sindh, Firozabad, Agra, Lahore, Multan Uch Sharif, and Karachi.

The lineage traces directly to Imam Ali al-Naqi al-Hadi (A.S), from the holy Ahl al-Bayt lineage of Prophet Muhammad ﷺ through Imam Hussain (A.S).

Archive Purpose

This page preserves the genealogical archive of the Naqvi Bukhari Sayyid families connected with Aale Ameer and Aale Nafees. It includes the direct lineage and listed collateral branches from Adam (A.S) to the Adnani Arab line, Quraysh, Ahl al-Bayt, Imam Ali al-Naqi al-Hadi (A.S), Bukhari Naqvi saints, and modern descendants in Hyderabad Sindh, Karachi, Agra, Firozabad, Lahore, Multan and Uch Sharif.

Migration Route

Arabia → Baghdad → Bukhara → Multan → Uch Sharif → Agra → Firozabad → Lahore → Hyderabad Sindh → Karachi.

Religious and Historical Identity

The family is recorded as Naqvi Bukhari Sayyid, connected to Imam Ali al-Naqi al-Hadi (A.S), Hazrat Jalaluddin Surkh Posh Bukhari (R.A), and later Aale Ameer and Aale Nafees branches. The archive records Ahl al-Bayt devotion, Ithna Ashari foundations, Sufi affiliations, and historical migration memories.

Migration History

The family lineage migrated historically through:

  • Arabia
  • Baghdad
  • Bukhara
  • Uch Sharif
  • Multan
  • Agra
  • Firozabad
  • Lahore
  • Hyderabad Sindh
  • Karachi

Religious and Spiritual Identity

Historically the family maintained strong devotion to Ahl al-Bayt traditions, Azadari, Islamic scholarship, and Sufi spiritual traditions connected to Chishti, Qadiri and Bukhari lineages.
